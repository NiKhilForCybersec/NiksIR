Skills Overview by Tier

SOC analyst skills build progressively from foundational (L1) to advanced (L3). This matrix helps you understand what's expected at each level and how to grow.

L1 Tier 1

Focus: Alert triage, initial assessment

  • Follow documented procedures
  • Basic tool operation
  • Escalation decisions
  • Clear documentation

L2 Tier 2

Focus: Deep investigation, incident handling

  • Complex analysis
  • Root cause determination
  • Remediation guidance
  • Procedure development

L3 Tier 3

Focus: Advanced threats, engineering

  • Threat hunting
  • Detection engineering
  • Malware analysis
  • Strategic improvements

Recommended Certifications

Tier Certifications Focus
L1
  • CompTIA Security+
  • CompTIA CySA+
  • SC-200 (Microsoft Security Operations)
 Foundational security concepts, SOC operations
L2
  • SANS GCIA (Intrusion Analyst)
  • SANS GCIH (Incident Handler)
  • BTL1 (Blue Team Level 1)
  • SC-200 + AZ-500
 Deep technical analysis, incident response
L3
  • SANS GREM (Reverse Engineering)
  • SANS GCFA (Forensic Analyst)
  • OSCP (Offensive Security)
  • BTL2 (Blue Team Level 2)
 Advanced analysis, threat hunting, malware
Experience Over Certs

Certifications validate knowledge, but hands-on experience matters more. Build home labs, participate in CTFs, and contribute to open-source projects.

Career Progression

Typical Progression Path

0-2 years
L1 SOC Analyst
Alert triage, learn tools, follow procedures
2-4 years
L2 SOC Analyst
Deep investigation, incident handling, mentor L1
4-6 years
L3 / Senior Analyst
Threat hunting, detection engineering, complex incidents
6+ years
Specialist / Lead
SOC Lead, Threat Hunter, Detection Engineer, IR Lead

  • Team management
