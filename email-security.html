Email Threat Landscape

Email remains the #1 attack vector for initial access. Understanding email security is essential for every SOC analyst.

Why Email?

Email is ubiquitous, trusted, and reaches users directly. Attackers exploit human psychology (urgency, authority, curiosity) to bypass technical controls.

Layer Function Examples

Email Threat Types

Threat Description Key Indicators
Credential Phishing Fake login pages to steal passwords Urgent language, login links, spoofed brands
Malware/Ransomware Malicious attachments or links Unexpected attachments, macros, executables
BEC (Business Email Compromise) Impersonate executive for financial fraud Wire transfer requests, urgency, secrecy
Spear Phishing Targeted attack using personal info Personalized content, researched details
Invoice Fraud Fake invoices with changed payment details Changed bank accounts, urgent payment
Thread Hijacking Reply to stolen email thread with malware RE: subject, legitimate conversation
QR Code Phishing Malicious QR codes in email QR codes linking to phishing sites
Thread Hijacking

Attackers steal email threads (via compromised accounts) and reply with malware. The email looks legitimate because it's part of a real conversation. Very effective.

Investigation Basics

Key Questions to Answer

  • Who received it? Single user or widespread campaign?
  • Did anyone click? Check URL click logs
  • Did anyone submit credentials? Check for successful logins from suspicious IPs
  • Did anyone open attachments? Check EDR for execution
  • Is the sender legitimate? Check authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC)
  • Is the domain malicious? Check reputation, registration date
