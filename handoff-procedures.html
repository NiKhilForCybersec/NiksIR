Handoff Procedures

All Tiers Section 2.6 ~10 min read
On This Page
  • Why Handoffs Matter
  • Handoff Process
  • Handoff Template
  • Best Practices

Why Handoffs Matter

Shift handoffs ensure continuity of operations. Poor handoffs lead to dropped incidents, duplicated work, missed context, and SLA breaches.

Poor Handoff Consequences

  • Active incidents forgotten
  • Investigation context lost
  • Same work done twice
  • SLA breaches
  • Stakeholders left waiting

Good Handoff Benefits

  • Seamless continuity
  • Context preserved
  • Clear ownership
  • No surprises
  • Professional operation

Handoff Process

Outgoing Shift Responsibilities

  1. Update all tickets: Current status, next steps documented
  2. Prepare handoff notes: Complete template
  3. Identify priorities: What needs immediate attention
  4. Verbal briefing: Walk through with incoming shift
  5. Transfer ownership: Reassign tickets as needed

Incoming Shift Responsibilities

  1. Arrive prepared: Allow time for handoff
  2. Review handoff notes: Read before verbal briefing
  3. Ask questions: Clarify anything unclear
  4. Accept ownership: Acknowledge assigned tickets
  5. Check queue: Review current alert status
Overlap Time

Schedule 15-30 minutes of shift overlap for proper handoffs. Handoffs conducted via notes alone without verbal briefing lose critical context.

Best Practices

  • Document throughout shift: Don't scramble at end
  • Be specific: "Follow up at 10am" not "follow up later"
  • Include context: Why things matter, not just what
  • Highlight blockers: What's preventing progress
  • Note stakeholder expectations: Who's waiting for updates
  • Verbal + written: Never rely on just one
  • Confirm understanding: Incoming shift acknowledges

During Active Incidents

Major incidents require enhanced handoffs:

  • Extended overlap time
  • Detailed timeline review
  • Introduction to incident commander
  • Access to war room / bridge
  • Clear role assignment
Key Takeaways
  • Good handoffs ensure operational continuity
  • Both outgoing and incoming have responsibilities
  • Use structured template for consistency
  • Include context, not just status
  • Verbal briefing + written notes together
  • Major incidents need enhanced handoff procedures
