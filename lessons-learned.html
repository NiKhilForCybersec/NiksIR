Lessons Learned

L2 L3 Section 10.6 ~10 min read
On This Page
  • Purpose
  • The Meeting
  • Key Questions

Purpose

The lessons learned (post-incident review, retrospective) is the final phase of incident response. It's where we turn incidents into improvements.

Goals

  • Understand what happened
  • Identify what worked well
  • Find gaps in detection/response
  • Improve processes and tools
  • Prevent recurrence

Not About

  • Blaming individuals
  • Punishing mistakes
  • Assigning fault
  • Creating fear
Blameless Culture

Effective lessons learned require a blameless approach. Focus on process and system improvements, not individual mistakes. People are more honest when they don't fear punishment.

The Meeting

When to Hold

  • Within 1-2 weeks of incident closure
  • While details are still fresh
  • After systems are stable and stress has reduced

Who Should Attend

Role Why
Incident responders Firsthand knowledge of what happened
SOC analysts involved Detection and initial response perspective
IT teams affected System recovery perspective
Management (as appropriate) Resource and policy decisions
Facilitator (neutral) Keep discussion productive

Meeting Structure

Suggested Agenda (60-90 minutes)

  1. Timeline review (15 min) - Walk through what happened
  2. What worked well (15 min) - Celebrate successes
  3. What could improve (20 min) - Identify gaps
  4. Root cause analysis (15 min) - Why did this happen
  5. Action items (15 min) - Specific improvements

Key Questions

Detection ‚ñº
  • How was the incident detected?
  • How long between compromise and detection?
  • Were there earlier indicators we missed?
  • What alerts fired? Which should have?
  • How can we detect this faster next time?
Response ‚ñº
  • Was the initial response appropriate?
  • Did we have the right tools and access?
  • Were runbooks/playbooks helpful?
  • Was escalation timely and effective?
  • What slowed us down?
Communication ‚ñº
  • Was communication clear and timely?
  • Did the right people get informed?
  • Were stakeholders kept updated?
  • Any communication breakdowns?
Containment & Recovery ‚ñº
  • Was containment fast enough?
  • Did containment cause unnecessary impact?
  • Was eradication complete?
  • How long did recovery take?
  • Were backups adequate?
Root Cause ‚ñº
  • What was the initial access vector?
  • Why was that vector available?
  • What vulnerabilities were exploited?
  • Were there control gaps?
  • How can we prevent this specific attack?

Documentation

Post-Incident Report Contents

  • Executive Summary - Brief overview for leadership
  • Timeline - Detailed chronology of events
  • Impact Assessment - What was affected, business impact
  • Root Cause - How the incident occurred
  • Detection Analysis - How/when discovered
  • Response Summary - Actions taken
  • What Worked Well - Successes to reinforce
  • Areas for Improvement - Gaps identified
  • Action Items - Specific remediation tasks
  • IOCs - Indicators for future detection

Report Audience

Audience Content Focus
Executive/Board Business impact, risk, high-level actions
Technical Teams Full technical detail, IOCs, detection rules
Legal/Compliance Timeline, data affected, regulatory impact
SOC/IR Team Playbook updates, detection improvements

Follow-up Actions

Common Improvement Areas

Category Example Actions
Detection New alert rules, improved logging, threat hunting queries
Prevention Patching, configuration changes, new controls
Process Updated playbooks, improved escalation, communication plans
Training User awareness, analyst skill development
Tooling New capabilities, better visibility, automation

Action Item Tracking

  • Assign owner to each action item
  • Set realistic due dates
  • Track completion status
  • Review in regular security meetings
  • Measure improvement in future incidents
Don't Skip This Phase

Under pressure to move on, teams often skip lessons learned. This is a mistake‚Äî without this phase, the same incidents repeat. Schedule it immediately after incident closure so it doesn't get forgotten.

Key Takeaways
  • Hold lessons learned within 1-2 weeks while details are fresh
  • Focus on process improvement, not blame
  • Include all stakeholders who were involved
  • Document both what worked AND what needs improvement
  • Create specific, assigned action items with due dates
  • Track action completion in regular meetings
  • Turn incidents into organizational improvements
