Insider Threat Types

Malicious Insider

Intentional harm

  • Data theft for personal gain
  • Selling information to competitors
  • Sabotage out of revenge
  • Espionage for foreign actors

Negligent Insider

Unintentional harm

  • Accidental data exposure
  • Ignoring security policies
  • Falling for phishing
  • Poor data handling practices

Compromised Insider

Account hijacked

  • Credentials stolen by attacker
  • Malware on endpoint
  • Social engineering victim
  • Acting under coercion
Investigation Approach

Initially, assume the insider may be compromised (not malicious). Investigate to determine intent before escalating as malicious insider. Many apparent insider threats are actually account compromises.

High-Risk Combinations â–¼

Multiple indicators together increase risk significantly:

  • Resigned + Large downloads: Stealing data before departure
  • PIP + After-hours access: Preparing for termination
  • Conflict + USB usage: Potential sabotage/theft
  • Financial issues + Accessing customer data: Selling information

Investigation Approach

Before You Begin
  • Get HR/Legal authorization for investigation scope
  • Confirm what data you're authorized to access
  • Document authorization and investigation boundaries
  • Do NOT alert the subject of investigation
Step 1: Baseline User Activity â–¼

Understand what's normal for this user:

  • What systems do they normally access?
  • What are their typical working hours?
  • What data do they legitimately need?
  • What devices do they use?
Step 2: Review Recent Activity â–¼

Look for deviations from baseline:

  • File access patterns (SharePoint, file shares)
  • Email activity (volume, external recipients)
  • Cloud application usage
  • Endpoint activity (USB, downloads)
  • Authentication patterns (times, locations)

Step 3: Focus on Sensitive Data â–¼
  • What sensitive files were accessed?
  • Any DLP policy violations?
  • Data classification of accessed content?
  • Was data downloaded, copied, or shared?
Step 4: Build Timeline â–¼

Construct chronological sequence of events:

  • When did suspicious activity begin?
  • Does it correlate with any life/work events?
  • Is there a pattern (daily, weekends)?
  • What was the progression?
Step 5: Assess Intent â–¼

Determine if activity appears malicious or accidental:

Suggests MaliciousSuggests Accidental/Negligent
Deliberate obfuscation (encryption, renaming)Single incident
Covering tracks (deleting logs)Immediately reported
Use of personal accounts/devicesFollowed by correction
Pattern of activity over timeWithin normal job scope
Accessing data outside job needReasonable explanation exists

Stakeholder Coordination

Stakeholder Role in Investigation When to Involve
HR Employment context, interview coordination, disciplinary action Before any investigation begins
Legal Legal risk assessment, evidence handling, litigation hold Before any investigation begins
Management Business context, user's manager for background With HR guidance
Physical Security Badge access review, building access If physical access relevant
Privacy/Compliance Data protection, regulatory requirements If regulated data involved
Do NOT
  • Interview the subject without HR/Legal guidance
  • Discuss investigation with unauthorized parties
  • Access data beyond authorized scope
  • Alert the subject through unusual account activity
  • Make assumptions about guilt or intent

Response Options

Response decisions are made by HR/Legal/Management, not SOC:

Option When Used Who Decides
Continue Monitoring Gather more evidence, unclear situation Security + HR
Enhanced Monitoring High-risk user, pre-termination period HR + Legal
Access Restriction Limit access to sensitive data/systems Management + HR
Interview/Confrontation Sufficient evidence, seek explanation HR + Legal
Account Suspension Immediate risk, pending investigation HR + Management
Termination Policy violation confirmed HR + Legal + Management
Law Enforcement Criminal activity, significant theft/fraud Legal + Executive
Playbook Summary
  1. Coordinate: Get HR/Legal authorization before investigating
  2. Baseline: Understand normal user behavior
  3. Investigate: Review activity, focus on sensitive data
  4. Timeline: Build chronological sequence
  5. Assess: Malicious vs. negligent vs. compromised
  6. Report: Provide findings to HR/Legal for decision
  7. Maintain confidentiality throughout
