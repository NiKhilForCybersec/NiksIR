Why Handoffs Matter

Effective shift handoffs ensure continuity of operations and prevent threats from falling through the cracks during transitions. A poor handoff can result in missed alerts, delayed response, or duplicate work.

Without Good Handoffs

  • Active incidents get dropped
  • Alerts sit unaddressed
  • Context is lost
  • Duplicate investigations
  • SLA breaches

With Good Handoffs

  • Seamless incident continuation
  • No gaps in coverage
  • Full context transferred
  • Efficient use of time
  • SLAs maintained

Handoff Components

Every shift handoff should cover these areas:

1. Active Incidents â–¼

Any incidents currently being worked or awaiting action:

  • Incident ID and Priority
  • Current Status: Investigation, containment, remediation, waiting
  • Summary: What happened, what's been done
  • Next Steps: What needs to happen next
  • Blockers: Anything preventing progress
  • Key Contacts: Who's involved (IT, business, external)
2. Alert Queue Status â–¼
  • Number of alerts pending review
  • Oldest alert age
  • Any high-priority alerts requiring immediate attention
  • Any alerts that have been looked at but need more work
3. Ongoing Campaigns/Threats â–¼
  • Active phishing campaigns targeting the organization
  • Known threats in the wild relevant to our environment
  • IOCs being actively monitored
  • Threat intelligence advisories
4. System Status â–¼
  • Any security tools with issues (SIEM, EDR, email gateway)
  • Planned maintenance windows
  • Known log gaps or ingestion issues
  • Any infrastructure issues affecting SOC operations
5. Notable Items â–¼
  • Important communications from management
  • Policy or procedure changes
  • Staffing notes (who's on call, who's out)
  • Anything unusual that incoming shift should know

Handoff Process

Standard Handoff Flow

-30 min
Outgoing: Prepare Handoff
Document active incidents, queue status, and notable items
-15 min
Overlap Begins
Incoming shift arrives, reviews handoff document
0 min
Verbal Handoff
Walk through active incidents and critical items together
+10 min
Q&A
Incoming shift asks questions, clarifies details
+15 min
Handoff Complete
Incoming shift assumes responsibility, outgoing departs
Overlap Period

Schedule at least 15-30 minutes of overlap between shifts. This allows for proper verbal handoff and questions. Never do a "relay race" handoff with no overlap.

P1 Incident Handoffs

For critical incidents, consider whether a handoff is appropriate or if the current analyst should continue. Changing analysts mid-crisis can lose momentum. Consult with leadership on major incident handoff decisions.

Key Takeaways
  • Handoffs are critical for 24/7 coverage continuity
  • Cover: active incidents, queue status, threats, systems, notable items
  • Always include both written documentation and verbal walkthrough
  • Schedule overlap time between shifts
  • Document throughout your shift, not just at the end
  • Confirm the incoming shift understands before leaving
