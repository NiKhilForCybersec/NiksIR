Why Metrics Matter

Metrics provide visibility into SOC performance, help justify resources, identify improvement areas, and demonstrate value to leadership. The right metrics drive the right behaviors.

Performance Tracking

Measure how well the SOC is detecting and responding to threats

Resource Justification

Data-driven arguments for staffing, tools, and budget

Process Improvement

Identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies

Analyst Development

Track individual and team growth

Metrics Can Mislead

Be careful what you measure‚Äîpeople optimize for metrics. Measuring only "tickets closed" might lead to rushing investigations. Balance speed metrics with quality metrics.

Core SOC Metrics

The essential metrics every SOC should track:

MTTD
Mean Time to Detect
MTTR
Mean Time to Respond
MTTC
Mean Time to Contain
MTTR
Mean Time to Remediate
Metric Definition Why It Matters Target Range
MTTD Time from threat occurrence to detection Measures detection capability effectiveness Minutes to hours (varies by threat)
MTTR (Respond) Time from alert to analyst response Measures SOC responsiveness P1: <15min, P2: <30min
MTTC Time from detection to containment Measures ability to stop active threats P1: <1hr, P2: <4hr
MTTR (Remediate) Time to fully resolve an incident Measures end-to-end response capability P1: <24hr, P2: <72hr
Dwell Time Time attacker is present before detection Overall security posture indicator Goal: <24 hours
Calculation Example

MTTD: If malware executed at 09:00 and alert fired at 09:05, MTTD = 5 minutes
MTTR: If alert fired at 09:05 and analyst began work at 09:12, MTTR = 7 minutes

Quality Metrics

Speed without quality is dangerous. Track these to ensure thorough work:

Metric Description How to Measure
Investigation Quality Score Completeness and accuracy of investigations Peer review, QA sampling
Documentation Completeness Are tickets properly documented? Checklist audit
Missed Detection Rate Threats found by other means (not SOC) Track discovery source
Re-opened Incidents Incidents reopened due to incomplete work Ticket tracking
Correct Classification Rate Accuracy of TP/FP/BTP determination QA review, L2 validation
SLA Compliance % of alerts handled within SLA Automated tracking
Quality Review Process

Implement regular ticket reviews where L2/L3 analysts review L1 work. This improves quality, provides coaching opportunities, and ensures consistent standards.

Common Metrics Pitfalls

Pitfall Problem Better Approach
Ticket Counting Analysts rush through tickets, miss things Balance with quality metrics
MTTR Only Fast closure without proper investigation Add investigation completeness score
Alert Volume Goals Creating alerts just to hit numbers Focus on meaningful detection
Ignoring FP Rate Drowning in noise, analyst burnout Track and actively reduce FPs
Individual Competition Analysts compete instead of collaborate Team metrics, peer support
Static Targets Gaming metrics once targets are known Evolve metrics over time
Key Takeaways
  • Track both speed (MTTD, MTTR) and quality metrics
  • Measure what matters, not just what's easy to count
  • Use benchmarks as guides, not absolute targets
  • Review metrics regularly and evolve them over time
  • Be aware of gaming‚Äîmetrics change behavior
  • Balance individual and team metrics
